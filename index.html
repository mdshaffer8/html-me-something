2017 Year in Review

Camping at Hog Island

February

In the beginning of the year, I took a weekend camping trip with by boyfriend, Sam, to Hog Island. We spent two nights Tent Camping, which is one of our favorite things to do! We canoed down the Withlacoochee River and took a break to eat some smoked mullet that we had bought from a roadside fish stand.

Trip to Everglades City

March

For Sam's Birthday we took a trip to Everglades City. It's the coolest small town I've ever been to. We stayed at the Rod and Gun Club, which is a historic lodge built in the 1890s. My favorite things to do here were visiting the Big Cypress Bend Boardwalk, taking an Airboat Tour through the mangroves, and taking a day road trip through Old Loop Road.

Puerto Rico Trip

April

Sam and I took a trip to Puerto Rico to celebrate a friend's birthday. Sam lived here before when he was in the Coast Guard, so he knew all the great spots to go to! We arrived early so we stayed the first night in Utuado, which is in the mountains. For the rest of the trip, we stayed in Isabela. We also got a chance to take a day trip to Old San Juan since our flight was delayed by several hours.

Ohio Trip

May

My son, Kai, and I went to Ohio to visit my oldest sister Shannon. All of the sisters (there are a total of 5 of us) met to have a weekend vacation together. Pictured here is Kai (with the glasses) and my nephew, Jack. Shannon lives in Rocky River, which is West of Cleveland.

RIP Scott Shaffer

June

My father died on June 10th. He was diagnosed with Stage IV Lung Cancer a month prior, on May 5th. It was quite a shock, because we had no idea his condition would get so bad so quickly. I recommend the following links for anyone dealing with a terminal illness:

  • Hospice
  • Palliative Care
  • Lung Cancer Awareness

Trip to Orlando

July

I took a short weekend trip to Orlando with my sister, my niece, and Kai. We stayed at a hotel with a lazy river! The kids got to have their own little room with a bunk bed. It was just a fun, spur of the moment idea and a great way to spend some quality time with the fam.

Accepted to LaunchCode!

October

I was thrilled and honored to be accepted to LaunchCode! I tell everyone I know about this program and am really proud to be a part of something so great! My dad has been in the technology industry since his days in the Navy and he would be equally excited if he was still here. My goal is to do well in the program and continue on to a successful career!

Thanksgiving Breakfast

November

This is Kai, Sam, and I with Sam's family. Sam and I recently moved to this house in Tampa, which is not too far from CareerSource. We hosted Thanksgiving breakfast for his family and went to my family's for Thanksgiving lunch! There are always many family events to go to because I have a big, extended family!
